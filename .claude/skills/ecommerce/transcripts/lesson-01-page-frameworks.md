# Lesson 01 — Page Frameworks & Voice

## Homepage / Landing Page

Work through the page top to bottom. Every section must either build interest or increase trust — cut anything that does neither.

1. **Hero** — headline answers "what is this and why should I care" in one sentence. Single CTA only. Full-width lifestyle image showing the product in use.
2. **Below-hero trust strip** — 3 icons that knock out the 3 biggest objections before the user scrolls.
3. **What is this** — one sentence or 3 bullets max. Plain language. If someone has to read it twice, rewrite it.
4. **Featured collection / best sellers** — show actual products early. Don't make people hunt.
5. **Features as benefits** — 2–3 features max. Each gets its own visual. Write the benefit, not the feature name. ("Sleep deeper" not "Temperature regulation").
6. **Who is this for** — 2–3 specific audience types, each with their own headline. If your product speaks to everyone, it speaks to no one.
7. **Social proof** — real names, real photos, specific results. No anonymous reviews. If you have zero reviews: put a satisfaction guarantee here instead — "try risk-free for 30 days" beats an empty section.
8. **Authority / credibility** — press, certifications, experts, or a founder story with a face and a reason.
9. **Final CTA** — close with a transformation reminder before the button. Not just a "buy now" — remind them what changes when they buy.

## About Page

1. Lead with WHY — what problem were you trying to solve, not your credentials.
2. Connect the mission to the customer — this is about what you believe in *together*, not a biography.
3. Human, not corporate — conversational tone, first person.
4. A real photo — even a simple one dramatically increases trust.
5. Social links — Instagram, shop link, wherever people can verify you're real.
6. Clear CTA at the end — email, contact form, trade program link, something.

## FAQ Page

1. Source questions from real customer inquiries, reviews, and forums — not what you wish they asked.
2. Use FAQs to handle objections, not just logistics. "Is this renter-friendly?" is an objection, not a logistics question.
3. Keep answers short and plainspoken.
4. Address emotional objections too — safety (kids, pets), commitment (returns, samples), compatibility (walls, rooms).
5. Update it as new questions arrive in your inbox.

## Product / Collection Page

1. Images first — lifestyle photo before product-on-white. Show it in a real room.
2. Headline = outcome, not product name.
3. Price anchored against the alternative ("vs. $800 real grasscloth").
4. Clear customization path — if it can be recolored or resized, say so prominently.
5. Samples CTA visible before the add-to-cart button — reduces purchase anxiety.
6. Reviews near the buy button, not buried at the bottom.

## Voice Rules

- Every section either builds interest or increases trust. Remove anything that does neither.
- Features → benefits. Always ask "what does this mean for the customer's life?"
- Specificity converts. "10,000 happy customers" beats "customers love us."
- One CTA per section. Never give someone three things to click.
- Hesitant browsers become buyers when trust accumulates fast enough. Trust = social proof + guarantee + real contact info + consistent branding.
