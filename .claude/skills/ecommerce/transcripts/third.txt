Here's something that most store owners get completely wrong.
They spend weeks on their product page and about 45 seconds on everything else.
And then they wonder why people are landing on their site and not buying anything.
But here's the truth, your product page gets people interested,
but it's the pages around it that actually makes them trust you enough
so they can buy something from you.
A store without an about us page, an FAQ or clear refund policy
doesn't build enough trust signals at all for someone to buy anything.
It looks like something that just appeared overnight
and might disappear just as fast with their money.
So today we're going to walk you through every single essential page
that your store needs, what needs to be on each one
and the trust signals that turn a hesitant browser into a confident buyer.
This stuff is not glamorous at all, but it's the difference
between a high converting store and a store where no one buys anything.
Let's do it.
So first your home page or landing page structure.
You want to think of your home page like a conversation.
It needs to take someone from what is this to I need to buy this
just from a single scroll.
So here's the structure that every single high converting landing page uses.
We'll use 8 Sleep as a live example
because we think they have one of the best converting landing pages we've seen.
Number one is your hero section.
This is simply the first thing people see as soon as they land on your website.
Your headline should immediately answer what your product is
and why someone should actually care.
That should all be in one sentence.
8 Sleep does this really well, for example.
They use something like Design for Deep Sleep personalized to you.
On your hero section, always have one CTA button only.
Just pick one action you want your potential customer to do,
not three links that take them different places.
Pick one action you want them to take and make it really obvious,
not three links competing for different attention.
Have a full width lifestyle image that actually shows the product getting used in a live setting.
Don't use a wide background product chart.
For example, 8 Sleep, they showed a pod in an actual bed getting used.
Now, right below the hero section,
you can have three trust icons that address the biggest objections that your customers might have.
For example, 8 Sleep does this really well
with showing their guarantees and their shipping policies
to make the customers actually trust them.
This takes the objections out of the way before someone even trolls.
The second thing you want is what your product actually is.
This is a super short section that explains what your product actually does in plain language.
Just use one sentence or three bullet points maximum.
This is not a feature list.
It's a simple answer to someone asking themselves,
okay, but how does this work?
For example, 8 Sleep does that with a single line
answering a question about their sleeping system working on any bed.
So here's a tip.
If someone has to read this section twice to understand your product, change it.
Now, by this point, you've built a little bit of trust
for the people browsing your website.
So what you can also have is a small collection showing your best sellers.
Right after that, you're going to want to have your features,
broken down into benefits.
Pick two or three most important product features
and give each one its own visual and a two-line explanation.
The key word here is benefits.
Not what the feature is, but what it actually means for a customer's life.
For example, 8 Sleep does that with temperature, elevation, and sound.
Each one has a clear visual and explanation of what it does.
One feature per visual.
Don't cram them all into one block.
The next section is who is this for?
So most beginners in e-commerce skip this part entirely,
but it's one of the biggest opportunities to actually stand out.
So identify two or three types of people
that would buy your product and speak directly to each one.
This will make every single reader feel like it was made for them.
So for example, 8 Sleep does this really well.
They identify couples, women who are pregnant, or even athletes.
Each of these types of buyers should have their own headline.
And remember, if your product speaks to everyone, you're speaking to no one.
Keep it specific.
The next section is literally the most important one.
You want to have social proof.
Testimonials or reviews.
Leave your strongest proof.
If you have stats or results or even reviews from your friends, add them in there.
Get them on the page with real names and real pictures.
Don't do anonymous pictures.
They don't convert.
You can also go a bit beyond that.
For example, 8 Sleep added clinical results about the product and how it helps people.
All you need is a few testimonials and reviews.
If you don't have any of these yet, simply ask your friends and families.
Send them your product and ask for a review.
If you're launching and have absolutely no reviews,
you can put your guaranteed front and center here.
Put something like, try risk-free for 30 days is way more powerful than having nothing in this section.
Shopify has built-in review apps.
Set one up now so they're ready whenever your first reviews come in.
The next section you really need is authority or credibility.
Something on the page should answer this question.
Why should I trust this brand?
This could be something like press mentions, certifications, or experts mentioning your product.
But I'm guessing most of you might not have that.
Most people like buying from a person, not a brand.
Most of you will have a product that speaks directly to your heart and purpose.
Leverage that and put a founder's story about why you're building this product.
As an example, 8 Sleep uses industry leaders in the sleep and health space.
And they have their testimonials right on their website.
And look, you don't need a famous neuroscientist to promote your product.
All you need is to show that you know what you're doing and you stand behind your product.
Finally, at the end of your homepage or landing page, you just want to have a final CTA.
Simply close the page with a full-width visual that reminds people about why they should buy it.
Not just a buy button, a reminder of the transformation right before the button.
Make it feel like a conclusion to a conversation, not an afterthought tacked out at the bottom.
As a golden rule, every single action or page on your website
should either be removed out or increased trust.
If a section is not doing one of these two things, don't add it.
So now that we're done with the main page,
we're going to talk about the accessory pages that help people build even more trust.
Like we talked in the beginning, the more trust you have, the more likely they are to buy.
So the first one you might need is an about us page.
Again, people like buying from people, not faceless businesses.
So you always want to lead with why.
Why did you build this?
What sparked the idea?
What makes it special?
Make it human.
For example, if you're the founder, add your face, tell your story and keep it conversational.
Don't make it corporate.
It's not a press release.
It's you from your heart telling people why you're building something.
Next, always connect the brand mission to the customer.
Remember, this is not about you.
It's about what you believe in together.
Always try to add a photo if possible.
Even a simple one will increase trust dramatically.
The next one is very simple.
It's your contact page.
You want to make it genuinely easy to reach you.
Add an email address, a contact form or even both.
Add an expected response time as well.
Adding something like we reply within 24 hours removes a lot of anxiety.
And if you have a location or a business address, always include it.
Even a registered business address will make a huge difference for your credibility.
And as an optional thing, you can always link your FAQs that people can self-serve before they reach out.
Then now as a transition, let's talk about your FAQ page.
You want to answer the questions most customers actually ask.
Go through reviews, forums or even your own inbox.
Your FAQ is also the biggest opportunity to actually sell to people.
You can use it to answer every single objection that your customer might have.
So always address the most common objections head on.
If people worry about X, answer with Y.
Keep your answers short and plain.
Make sure to always update it regularly as new questions come in.
And lastly, we're going to talk about trust signals.
These are all many things you can implement across your whole store that will improve trust.
So make sure you have secure badges at checkout.
Visa, MasterCard, Google Pay, Apple Pay.
Any single successful store that you'll see online will clearly show all these icons
both at their checkout and in their footer at the end.
Then also have a money-back guarantee badge.
You want to have it visible on your product page and at checkout.
Never bury it in the footer.
Also have real contact information.
An actual email and real response time shows people that you're actually a real business.
Like we already covered, always have your social proof like testimonials and reviews.
And make sure your branding, colors, fonts, etc. are consistent across every page.
And consistency will make you look like an amateur.
And then last one, make sure you have fast loading speed.
A slow site will kill your trust before customers are even seen it.
If your website is slow, compress your images before uploading them.
